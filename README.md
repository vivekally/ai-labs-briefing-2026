# The State of the Labs — July 2026

A field briefing on 22 frontier AI labs across the US, UK/Europe, Canada, and China:
what each lab is actually building, the five fault lines where they contradict each
other, the six points where they quietly agree, and the researchers betting more than
a billion dollars that LLMs are a dead end.

## Viewing

Open [`index.html`](index.html) in any browser — it is fully self-contained (no
external assets). The report is organized as tabs (one per section, deep-linkable
via URL hash, e.g. `index.html#dissenters`) with a light/dark toggle in the header
that also follows your system theme. If GitHub Pages is enabled for this repo, the
report is served at the Pages URL directly.

## Contents

- **§1 Executive summary** — five key findings
- **§2 The lab atlas** — 22 lab profiles grouped by region and doctrine
- **§3 Fault lines** — five positioning spectrums (open vs. closed, scaling vs. new
  architectures, AGI timelines, commercialization, business model) plus three ironies
- **§4 Common ground** — what every lab now agrees on
- **§5 The dissenters** — who is against LLMs, their technical objections, and their
  funded alternatives
- **§6 Method & sources** — scope, caveats, and 30+ principal sources

Graphics: an eight-month event timeline, doctrine distribution, the API price war,
OpenRouter token-share composition, the compute buildout in gigawatts, and the
dissenters' funding — all theme-aware, no external assets.

## Caveats

Compiled 13 July 2026 from public reporting and fact-checked the same day against
primary and news sources (the corrections pass fixed SSI's total raised, Thinking
Machines' valuation, Cohere's North GA date, and refreshed all model names to their
July 2026 state). Valuations, GPU counts, and model specifications are press-reported
and not independently verified. Doctrine placements are editorial judgment. Treat as
a dated snapshot, not a standing reference.
